The Turn of the Tide

A Journey from Lost to Found Through Faith

Arthur Gordon found himself in a terrifying state of being lost. Days passed where faith abandoned him, everything felt trivial and meaningless. His enthusiasm for work vanished, his writing became fruitless.

In desperation, he consulted a physician who found no physical illness. Instead, the doctor prescribed something extraordinary: a single day at the place of his happiest childhood memories, with four sealed envelopes to open at specific times...

9:00 AM
"Listen carefully and pay attention"
At first skeptical, Gordon opened his ears to the ocean. He heard waves, birds, then deeper sounds. The sea taught him patience, respect, interdependence. He listened not just to sounds but to silence itself. Tranquility began flowing within him.
12:00 PM
"Try to reach back with your memory"
Gordon recalled his furthest childhood memories, the happy moments without worry, brief instances of joy. Through this remembering, warmth and self-confidence began growing in his depths, reconnecting him with who he once was.
3:00 PM
"Examine your motives"
He delved deep into his motivesâ€”success, excellence, comfortâ€”realizing these weren't enough. He discovered: "If a person's motives are wrong, nothing right can come from them. If your concern is helping yourself rather than others, you won't do your work well. But serve others, driven by higher values, and you'll perform excellently."
6:00 PM
"Write your worries on the sand"
Gordon wrote his worries in the sand with a broken shell. As he walked away, the tide came in and erased every word. His worries, once so permanent in his mind, were swept away by nature's rhythm.
